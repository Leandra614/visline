ROV RESEARCH

About

The California Department of Fish and Wildlife uses robotic technology in the form of Remotely Operated Vehicle (ROV) surveys to conduct research in and outside of Marine Protected Areas (MPAs). ROV surveys are being conducted to gather baseline ecological conditions and status of managed species in California’s MPAs.

From the shoreline, it is hard to imagine the expanse below the water. Much of this oceanic expanse is at depths that make exploration and research very difficult. Most scuba diving for research occurs shallower than 100 feet which limits direct human observation to a narrow band of habitats near shore. Scuba research is also constricted by safety factors including the time allotted underwater and presence of dangerous conditions. ROV technology offers a safe and innovative option for exploring the deeper ocean.

The ROV is deployed off an ocean research vessel, with a group of scientists, technicians, and boat crew aboard. The ROV is “flown” by remote control via its tether and the vessel followed using an acoustic tracking system. Virtual video swath transects are preplanned using bathymetry maps and then flown by the ROV pilot who navigates using a live mapping computer screen of the acoustic tracking system and shipboard GPS.
